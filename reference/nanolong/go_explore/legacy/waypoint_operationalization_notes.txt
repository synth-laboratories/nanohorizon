Waypoint Operationalization Notes

Reference
- `references/myers_et_al_2025_horizon.pdf`
- `references/myers_et_al_2025_horizon.txt`

High-level takeaway
- The paper studies long-horizon behavior through waypoint-conditioned competence rather than only end-goal success.
- For our design, that is useful as evidence that intermediate structure matters, but we are not planning to expose waypoints to the agent as explicit subgoals.

What "operationalizing waypoints" means in our variant
- A waypoint is not just an intuitive subgoal description; it becomes an optimizer-side semantic label for post-hoc trace interpretation, archive organization, and rollout targeting.
- The agent is still run on the original task or end-goal instruction plus normal runtime context. In v0, waypoints are not emitted as intermediate instructions.
- This turns waypoint quality into something measurable through optimizer utility: do the waypoint labels improve checkpoint choice, frontier expansion, and reusable segment discovery?

Useful concepts for our Python Go-Explore variant
- Horizon generalization:
  Competence on shorter-horizon verified transitions should transfer to longer-horizon tasks.
- Waypoint-level evaluation:
  The right score object is not only per-seed return; it is performance across waypoint/subskill slices computed from post-hoc labels.
- Planning invariance from the paper is useful background:
  it is evidence that decompositions matter, but it is not the primary evaluation setup for our v0 because we are not training a waypoint-conditioned policy.

How this maps to our design
- Ontology learning via Helix should produce candidate task decompositions:
  skills, subtasks, entities, dependencies, and plausible waypoint chains.
- Waypoints/cells/checkpoints should be first-class archive items:
  `cell = checkpoint + ontology slice + waypoint labels + evidence`.
- Candidate evaluation should be Pareto over waypoint metrics computed after the rollout, not just over seeds.
- Checkpoint-targeted rollouts should be judged by whether they produce more verified progress, depth, frontier expansion, or reusable segments than fresh-start exploration.

Practical metrics to track
- waypoint completion rate
- waypoint reachability from checkpoint
- late-waypoint depth reached
- checkpoint progress gain over fresh-start rollouts
- subskill coverage across ontology-defined capability clusters
- robustness of waypoint completion across multiple checkpoints, not just one seed

Implication for first implementation
- Start with a prompt-optimization overlay:
  reuse GEPA-style candidate search, but replace seed-vector selection with waypoint-vector selection.
- Keep the interfaces modular so later policy-improvement plugins can reuse the same archive:
  online SFT, distillation, GRPO/Tinker, or other on-policy updates.

Why this matters for long-horizon LLM agents
- Seeds are often a poor summary for long-horizon competence because success is compositional.
- Waypoints operationalize "what was learned" in a reusable form for the optimizer and archive.
- Checkpoints make those waypoints actionable without exposing them to the agent: the optimizer can branch from them, test continuation policies, and gather targeted evidence.
